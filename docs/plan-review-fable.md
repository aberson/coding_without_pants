## Pressure-tested judgment calls

1. **Scope — both loops in v1: KEEP.** The Channel Loop (Steps 1–5) is low-complexity CRUD + templating; cutting the Pantsless Build would ship a generic content-CLI that never tests the channel's actual premise — M2 *is* the pilot's core, and the plan's own priority order puts the kid loop first. The over-build risk is real but lives in the edges, not a loop: cut `--record` (undeclared dep, untested), keep `/cwp-*` skills deferred as planned. The complexity concentration is Steps 8–9 — fix their spec (BLOCKERs above) rather than deferring them.
2. **Generate→verify→repair as reliability core: right architecture, currently unimplementable as written.** The loop shape (static + headless + evidence-fed repair + atomic commit + needs_human) is correct and well-bounded. But the static-check spec is self-falsifying (`</script>`, SVG xmlns), the behavioral check's pixel-diff branch is defeated by the contract's own mandated animation, the audio pitfall it names is invisible to every listed check, and one click cannot witness "can't break it". With the fixes above (context-scoped patterns, `data-action-count` hook, AudioContext shim + autoplay launch arg, N-click mash, single-defect calibration fixtures), the verifier genuinely keeps a *crashing/malformed/wrong-motif* toy off camera. What it still can't prove: "enjoys it" and fine-grained comprehension — correctly left to the human Pantsless Test.
3. **faster-whisper small/medium, ~25% WER as noisy-by-design: sound.** Right engine (CTranslate2, no PyTorch, on-device privacy), right posture (distill recovers intent; `kid_quote` keeps the comedy). Improvements, not replacements: pin default `small` with `--model medium` escalation, `language="en"`, `vad_filter=True`, `initial_prompt` vocabulary seeding (a real WER lever for child speech), and a pinned low-confidence threshold. Verify the ffmpeg prereq is actually needed (PyAV likely covers decode).
4. **Step slicing: mostly right, one real ordering bug.** The graph is topologically fine for its stated edges, but the SoT seeds (voice.md/build-contract.md) are produced in Step 10 *after* their consumers (Steps 4, 9) — split Step 10. Add M1.5 (solo live dry-run) between M1 and M2. Step 8 needs Depends-on: 7 once the brief format is pinned. Step 2 is the largest slice but acceptable.
5. **Acceptance bars M1/M2: falsifiable and right-sized** after two touches — reword M1's "using only cwp" to scope to cwp-owned stages, and insert M1.5 so the first live claude+whisper+verifier run doesn't spend the kid's patience on plumbing.

## VERDICT: NEEDS REVISION

The architecture is right — two loops over flat files, a calibrated verifier as the reliability spine, privacy-by-locality for audio, and an honest anti-over-build posture — and nothing found requires a redesign; every fix is a plan edit. But five blockers stand between this document and a safe `/plan-expedite`: the brief.md → verifier chain (the plan's own named SoT chain) is missing both its serialization and its must_haves→assertion mechanism, the verifier's static-check spec fails every valid toy as literally written, the SoT seed files are produced two steps after they're consumed, and the "public-repo-safe by construction" claim is convention-not-construction for the one artifact class most likely to carry a child's real name. **Fix first: (1)** pin the brief.md serialization + a closed, machine-checkable must_haves vocabulary with contract-mandated hooks (`data-action-count`, AudioContext shim) — this one fix resolves the verifier's false-pass/false-fail cluster and makes Steps 7–9 fresh-context buildable; **(2)** re-order the SoT seeds (split Step 10) and pin the static-check spec so Steps 4/8/9 build against real inputs instead of invented stand-ins; **(3)** close the kid-privacy text gap (gitignore `capture/`, redact-names guard, publish checklist) before the repo ever goes public. With those landed — plus the cheap M1.5 live dry-run — this is a well-shaped v1 that earns its own acceptance bar.
